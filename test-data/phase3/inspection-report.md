## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The property at 4217 Maplewood Drive is a 1987-built colonial-style single-family home, approximately 2,140 sq ft, 3 bedrooms, 2.5 bathrooms, with an attached 2-car garage and partially finished basement. The home is in typical condition for its age and shows evidence of generally adequate maintenance, with several deferred maintenance items and a few conditions that warrant attention prior to purchase.

**Items requiring immediate attention or specialist evaluation:**

1. **(FE) Roof** — Asphalt shingles are at or near end of serviceable life (est. 25–27 years old). Granule loss is significant in multiple zones. Recommend evaluation by licensed roofing contractor for replacement timeline and cost.
2. **(FE) Electrical Panel** — Main panel is a 150A Federal Pacific Stab-Lok panel. This brand has a documented history of breaker failure to trip under fault conditions. Recommend evaluation and likely replacement by licensed electrician.
3. **(R) Basement Water Intrusion** — Efflorescence and staining on the northeast foundation wall indicates past or active water intrusion. Sump pump is present and operational but shows signs of recent heavy use. Recommend evaluation by waterproofing specialist.
4. **(R) HVAC** — Gas furnace is 19 years old (2007 manufacture date), past typical serviceable life of 15–20 years. Functioning at time of inspection but replacement should be budgeted in near term.
5. **(R) Deck** — Ledger board attachment to house shows inadequate flashing, and several deck boards are soft underfoot with visible rot at board ends. Handrail does not meet current code height requirements.

**Estimated Age:** 25–27 years

## SECTION 4 — ELECTRICAL

**Service:** 150 amp overhead service entrance
**Panel:** Federal Pacific Electric (FPE) Stab-Lok, 150A, 30 circuit
**Wiring:** Copper throughout (confirmed in accessible areas)
**GFCIs Present:** Kitchen, bathrooms, garage — yes
**AFCIs Present:** Bedrooms — no (not required at time of build, now recommended)

**Observations:**
- Federal Pacific Stab-Lok panels have been the subject of significant industry scrutiny. Breakers in these panels have a documented failure rate — they may not trip under overcurrent or fault conditions, creating a fire risk. This panel type is flagged by most insurers and many buyers choose to replace proactively.
- One double-tapped breaker observed (two conductors on a single breaker terminal that is not rated for it) — a code violation requiring correction.
- Outlet in garage is ungrounded (2-prong) — recommend updating to 3-prong GFCI outlet.
- Exterior outlet on back deck has no cover plate — recommend weatherproof cover.

**Rating: FE — Electrician evaluation strongly recommended. Panel replacement likely. Estimate $1,500–$3,500 for panel upgrade.**

## SECTION 10 — DECK

**Type:** Pressure-treated wood, attached to rear of house, approximately 12x16 ft

**Observations:**
- Ledger board (attachment point to house): flashing is inadequate — no metal flashing visible, gap between ledger and siding allows water intrusion. This is a significant issue; improper ledger attachment is a leading cause of deck failure and can compromise house structure.
- Deck boards: 4–5 boards show soft spots underfoot, visible rot at ends and near ledger. Remaining boards appear serviceable but have weathered significantly.
- Posts and joists: appear structurally sound with no visible rot.
- Handrail: height measured at 32" — current IRC code requires 36" minimum. Does not meet code.
- Stair stringers: adequate, but no graspable handrail on stairs.

**Rating: R — Recommend licensed contractor evaluate ledger flashing and deck boards. Partial rebuild likely needed. Handrail must be brought to code.**
